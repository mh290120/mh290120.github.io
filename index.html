Welcome

I am currently pursuing a PhD in Mathematics at Dalhousie University, where my research focuses on combinatorial commutative algebra. I also serve as an Associate Professor of Mathematics at Government College University Lahore and have more than 15 years of experience in university teaching, research, graduate supervision, and academic leadership. Previously, I was a Visiting Postdoctoral Fellow and Adjunct Assistant Professor at Tulane University (2019–2020).

My research interests lie at the intersection of commutative algebra and combinatorics, with particular emphasis on monomial ideals, simplicial complexes, and their algebraic, combinatorial, and topological aspects. Through my role as Co-Manager of the Nova Scotia Math Circles Program, I contribute to mathematics outreach and help foster mathematical curiosity among school students across Nova Scotia. My work reflects a strong commitment to advancing mathematical research, fostering student learning, and promoting mathematics through outreach and community engagement.
